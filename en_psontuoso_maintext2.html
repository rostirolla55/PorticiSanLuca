Here are the key details about this architectural masterpiece:

1. Architecture and Symbolism

Built in 1674 , the arch was designed by architect Gian Giacomo Monti .

  • Structure: It appears as a triumphal arch of the Corinthian order, monumental and decidedly taller and more ornate than the remaining 665 arches of the portico.

  • Function: It marks the transition from the "profane" world of the city to the "sacred" world of the devotional journey. It marks the beginning of a nearly 4-kilometer-long path that symbolizes the body of the serpent (the devil) crushed at the feet of the Madonna (the Sanctuary).

2. Why is it called "Sumptuous"?

The name is not a modern exaggeration, but derives from its decorative richness compared to the sobriety of the rest of the route.

  • It is adorned with the coat of arms of the Bonaccorsi family , who financed its construction.

  • It features Latin inscriptions and relief decorations celebrating the protection of the Virgin over the city of Bologna.

3. A Historical Curiosity

Although today we see it perfectly integrated into the urban fabric, at the time of its construction, the Portone Sontuoso posed a real engineering and logistical challenge. It had to connect the flat stretch of road (built between 1674 and 1676) with the start of the steep climb leading to Meloncello.
