The link between the Portico of San Luca in Bologna and Carl'Antonio Facci (or Carlo Antonio Facci) concerns the funding and devotion that allowed the construction of this imposing architectural work.

Here are the main details:

  • Who was Carl'Antonio Facci: He was a wealthy Bolognese silk merchant who had his shop on Via delle Chiavature, in the heart of Bologna's historic center.

  • Contribution to the Portico: Like many other wealthy citizens, corporations, and noble families of the time, Facci contributed financially to the construction of the portico. His name is particularly linked to a section of the Portico di Pianura (the flat stretch from Via Saragozza to the Arco del Meloncello).

  • The inscription: Near Arch 101 (according to some historical cataloguings, such as the Catalogo de' Devoti ), there is or was mentioned an inscription that reads: "DEL SIGNOR CARL'ANTONIO FACCI. 1779" . This date indicates the period in which the merchant offered his contribution for the construction or completion of that specific section.

  • Historical context: The Portico of San Luca, nearly 4 km long and composed of 666 arches, was built between 1674 and 1793 almost entirely thanks to private donations . The participation of people like Carl'Antonio Facci testifies to the deep devotion of the people of Bologna to the Madonna of San Luca and the desire to contribute to a work that is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

In short, Carlo Antonio Facci was not an architect, but one of the generous (devoted) financiers who made it possible to build the longest portico in the world as an act of faith and civic pride.
